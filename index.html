§ 02 — Life

Fifty-Five Years.
Eleven Lucid.

1844

Born in Röcken

Son of a Lutheran pastor in Saxon Prussia. His father dies of a brain ailment before Friedrich turns five — a shadow that follows him.

Studies under Friedrich Ritschl at Leipzig. Discovers Schopenhauer. Encounters Wagner. The two forces that will define and then betray him.

1865

Leipzig · Schopenhauer

1869

Chair at Basel — Age 24

Appointed full Professor of Classical Philology without completing his doctorate. The youngest ever. The establishment will not forgive The Birth of Tragedy.

Illness forces him to resign. Migraines, failing eyesight, nausea. He begins his wandering years — Sils-Maria, Nice, Genoa, Turin — writing in stolen hours between pain.

1879

Resignation · Wandering

1883–85

Zarathustra

Written in four bursts of ten days each. The Übermensch descends from the mountain. Almost no one reads it. Nietzsche publishes the fourth part at his own expense.

In the Piazza Carlo Alberto, Turin, he collapses — throws his arms around a flogged horse. He writes one last letter signing himself "Dionysus" and "The Crucified." The hammer falls silent.

1889

Turin · Collapse

1900

Weimar · Silence

Dies in Weimar at fifty-five. The twentieth century, which he had seen coming, begins. His sister, Elisabeth, curates his legacy with devastating distortions.

§ 03 — Philosophy

The Six
Hammers

i.   1882

Ewige
Wiederkunft

Eternal Recurrence

Live as though this moment — exactly this — must repeat infinitely. Not cosmology, but the heaviest test of life-affirmation ever conceived.

ii.   posth.

Wille
zur Macht

Will to Power

Physical0%
Intellectual0%
Creative0%

Not domination — growth, overcoming, the discharge of strength. Hover to assert your will.

iii.   1883

Über-
mensch

The Overman

Man is a rope across the abyss — a goal, not a stopping place. Not a race, but a demand: overcome yourself.

iv.   1887

Nihilismus

Nihilism

Not his final word — his diagnosis. When God dies and metaphysics collapses, the void must be confronted. Nietzsche's task was to survive the abyss and build on its ruins.

"Nihilism stands at the door: whence comes this uncanniest of all guests?"
v.   1882

Amor
Fati

Love of Fate

Not stoic endurance — radical love. Every sorrow, every mistake, every wound: wanted, affirmed, embraced.

"My formula for greatness: amor fati — that one wants nothing to be different."
vi.   1886

Jenseits von
Gut und Böse

Beyond Good and Evil

Morality is not discovered — it is created. Every moral system is a symptom of the psyche that produced it. The philosopher's task: revalue all values.

Three Metamorphoses of the Spirit — Thus Spoke Zarathustra, I.1

🐪

The Camel

Burden · Reverence · Thou Shalt

The spirit that kneels and willingly takes the heaviest loads — duty, morality, tradition. "What is difficult? asks the loadbearing spirit."

🦁

The Lion

Freedom · Destruction · I Will

The spirit that roars against the dragon "Thou Shalt" and tears away inherited values. It cannot create — but it can clear the ground.

👶

The Child

Creation · Play · New Beginning

Innocence and forgetting, a new beginning, a game, a self-propelling wheel. The child creates new values — the sacred "Yes" to life.

§ 06 — Legacy

The Reverb
of the Hammer

Existentialism

Sartre · Camus · Heidegger

The idea that existence precedes essence — that humans must create their own meaning — is Nietzsche's legacy rewritten in the shadow of two world wars.

Postmodernism

Foucault · Derrida · Deleuze

Perspectivism and the genealogical method become the tools of deconstruction. Nietzsche's suspicion of grand narratives echoes through every postmodern critique.

Literature & Art

Kafka · Yeats · Mann

The Übermensch haunts modernist fiction. Thomas Mann's Doctor Faustus is a direct confrontation. Kafka's alienation owes more to Nietzsche than to any sociologist.

Music

Strauss · Mahler · Delius

Richard Strauss's Also sprach Zarathustra (1896) made Nietzsche's vision audible. Mahler set his words. The Dionysian impulse entered the concert hall.

Misappropriation

The Nazi Distortion

Elisabeth Förster-Nietzsche's editorial manipulations handed a profound individualist philosopher to fascist ideology. Nietzsche, who despised German nationalism and antisemitism, could not defend himself.

Counter-Culture

1960s · Punk · Silicon Valley

From the hippies questioning authority to punk's nihilistic energy to Silicon Valley's "move fast and break things" — Nietzsche's self-overcoming appears in unexpected clothes.
